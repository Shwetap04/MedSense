Title: Migraine – Triggers, Symptoms, and Management

Overview:
A migraine is a neurological condition causing intense throbbing head pain, sensitivity to light/sound, and nausea. It may last from hours to days.

Common Triggers:
- Stress or emotional changes
- Hormonal fluctuations (menstruation)
- Skipping meals
- Dehydration
- Excess caffeine
- Strong smells or bright lights
- Poor sleep
- Certain foods (aged cheese, chocolate, alcohol)

Symptoms:
- Throbbing/pulsating headache (one or both sides)
- Nausea and vomiting
- Sensitivity to light, sound, or smells
- Visual disturbances (aura)
- Fatigue

Self-Care:
- Rest in a dark, quiet room.
- Hydrate and eat light meals.
- Apply a cold compress.
- Maintain consistent sleep.
- Keep a migraine diary to identify triggers.

Prevention:
- Follow a regular sleep and meal schedule.
- Reduce screen time and harsh lighting.
- Manage stress through relaxation techniques.

Red Flags:
- First severe headache after age 40
- Sudden onset “worst headache of your life”
- Weakness or difficulty speaking
