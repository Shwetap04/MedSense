Title: Posture-Related Back & Neck Pain – Causes and Prevention

Overview:
Posture pain occurs due to prolonged sitting, slouching, or improper ergonomics. It is extremely common among students, office workers, and people with sedentary routines.

Causes:
- Slouching while sitting or standing
- Long hours at a desk without breaks
- Poor chair or desk height
- Forward head posture (from phones/laptops)
- Weak core muscles

Symptoms:
- Dull or sharp back pain
- Neck stiffness
- Shoulder tightness
- Headaches (from neck strain)
- Fatigue from muscle overuse

Self-Care:
- Stretch every 45–60 minutes.
- Adjust chair height so feet touch the floor.
- Keep monitor at eye level.
- Do simple neck & shoulder mobility exercises.
- Strengthen core muscles.

Red Flags:
- Pain radiating to arms or legs
- Tingling or numbness
- Severe pain after injury
