Overview:
Menstrual cramps occur due to contractions in the uterus. They can range from mild discomfort to severe pain.

Causes:
- Hormonal changes (prostaglandins)
- Stress and poor sleep
- Heavy menstrual flow
- Underlying conditions like PCOS or endometriosis (less common)

Symptoms:
- Lower abdominal pain
- Back pain
- Fatigue
- Nausea
- Mood swings

Self-Care:
- Use a heating pad on the lower abdomen.
- Stay hydrated.
- Light stretching or yoga.
- Avoid caffeine and salty foods.

Red Flags:
- Severe pain sudden in onset
- Pain interfering with daily tasks
- Irregular cycles or excessive bleeding
